## The one hard constraint (non-negotiable — HIPAA)

**No clinical PHI ever enters Stripe** — not in descriptions, line items, or
metadata. Payment processing sits under HIPAA's payment exception ONLY if PHI
never crosses into Stripe. Line items read generic ("Professional services");
the diagnosis/CPT/clinical detail stays in our DB, joined by our own invoice
id, never sent. This pairs with the Stripe guard in NYS-11.

## Scope

- Stripe Checkout session per invoice; webhook (`app/api/stripe/webhook` —
  the inbound handler already exists) reconciles payment → our `payments`
  table → `sendPaymentReceiptEmail`.
- Superbill PDF: the out-of-network reimbursement document the patient
  submits to their payer. This one DOES carry clinical codes — but it's
  generated by US, delivered to the PATIENT (portal download / email link),
  and never touches Stripe. Keep the two flows firewalled.
- Launch posture per the research doc: cash-pay / superbill is the likely v1;
  insurance-billing decision deferred.
