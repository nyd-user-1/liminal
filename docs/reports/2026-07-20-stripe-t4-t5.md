## T5 — notifications

`lib/email/stripe-notifications.ts`: `sendPaymentReceipt`, `sendTherapistPaid`,
`sendDisputeAlert`, `sendOnboardingNudge`. All return `Promise<boolean>` and
never throw — a `false` means "email not configured", not "payment failed".

Reuses the branded shell + Resend client in `lib/email.ts` rather than forking a
second template. That required exporting `shell` and `esc` there — a two-word
diff, the entire change to that shared file. No new provider.

Copy decisions worth a ruling:

- The **client receipt omits the platform fee**. The client paid one practice
  one amount; the split is our arrangement with the therapist. The therapist
  email states gross/fee/net plainly — a therapist who can't see what was
  withheld can't trust the number.

- The **nudge links to in-app onboarding, never an Account Link**. Those URLs
  are single-use and short-lived, so an emailed one arrives dead (brief
  §Hard guardrails).

**Two defects found by rendering the emails, which typecheck could not catch:**

- `due_by` was formatted through the server's local zone, shifting a
  midnight-UTC dispute deadline to the previous day — it printed "August 2" for
  an August 3 deadline. Now formatted in UTC. Same hazard `isoDateOnly` already
  guards against; worth knowing it recurs anywhere we render a Stripe instant.

- Dispute reasons printed as raw enums, and Stripe's e-commerce vocabulary is
  nonsense against a session: `product_not_received` now reads "Client says the
  session did not happen." Unknown codes fall back to de-snaked text.

## T4 — portal pay surface

The pay surface already existed (`PortalInvoiceSheet` + `/portal/invoices/[id]/pay`),
so this re-points it at the Connect contract rather than rebuilding it.

**Pay** now tries `POST /api/checkout/session` first. That route answers 409
when the therapist has no active connected account and 503 when the environment
has no Stripe key — both fall back to the existing platform path. So the portal
works at every stage of onboarding and upgrades itself when a therapist goes
live, with no flag to flip. Other statuses surface as errors rather than
retrying a call that would fail identically.

**Paid state no longer comes from the redirect.** `?paid=1` only means Stripe
took the card; a destination charge is marked paid by the webhook, which can
land after the browser does. The return leg shows a "confirming" Banner and
polls our own data (10 × 2s), then either confirms for real or says it is still
processing. The invoice id rides through the redirect in `sessionStorage`;
storage failures degrade to a generic wait, never a failed payment.

Previously the page toasted "Payment received — thank you!" the instant Stripe
redirected — asserted for a payment that had not been recorded.
