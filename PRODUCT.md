# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary (the home page's job): New Yorkers seeking mental health care.** Often anxious, overwhelmed, or in a hard moment; short on patience for phone tag and waitlists. They need to trust the care is real and licensed, confirm it takes their insurance, and get to a booked appointment quickly. Their context is a phone at night or a laptop between obligations.

**Secondary: New York clinicians and practices to recruit** onto Liminal's practice-management + EHR platform. They care about a full calendar, less paperwork, and a directory that sends them clients. On the home page they get one concentrated band; depth lives on the dedicated provider page.

## Product Purpose

Liminal is an all-in-one mental-health practice-management + EHR platform, fronted by a public directory of 116k+ New York providers. The home page's success = a patient searches, finds an in-network provider, and books same-week; secondarily, a provider clicks through to join. It exists to make finding real, covered, same-week mental health care feel possible instead of exhausting.

## Brand Personality

**Hushed, grounded, assured.** Calm authority — this is healthcare, not a wellness app, so credibility and clarity come first — carried with genuine tenderness, because the moment someone reaches out is vulnerable. Warmth is carried by the warm paper ground, the watercolor light, and one amber accent — working together so the paintings dissolve into the page.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS hero-metric template** and identical icon-heading-text card grids.
- **Braided dual-audience pages** that ping-pong patient → provider → patient.

## Design Principles

1. **Patient-first clarity.** The core job — find a covered provider, book same-week — is the loudest thing on the page. One job per fold.
2. **The watercolor light IS the brand.** Imagery and one amber accent carry all the warmth; the ground stays disciplined so the paintings glow.
3. **Assurance and tenderness together.** Healthcare credibility (grotesque, clean structure, in-network proof) married to human softness (serif voice, quiet pacing).
4. **Distinctive through color, type, and light — not decoration.** No eyebrows, no numbered scaffolding, no card reflex.
5. **One provider band, not a braid.** The provider story gets a single strong moment and a link out; it never interrupts the patient narrative twice.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA throughout. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 against its ground (the old muted-gray-on-tint was a known risk); large/display text ≥ 3:1; placeholder text held to the same bar. Every entrance animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` crossfade/instant fallback. Legible reading sizes and 65–75ch measures for prose. Focus-visible states on all interactive elements; alt text written in the brand's voice.
